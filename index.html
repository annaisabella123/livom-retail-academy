How to submit a good request

A complete request saves time — for you and the internal team.

  1. 1
    Name the topic clearly
    Not "delivery problem" — but "Delivery order #4821, Harvey 3-seater, Nova Sand, missing for 5 days".
  2. 2
    Include the order number
    Without this, no team can act quickly. Always attach it when available. Format: DE-#, CH-#, AT-#, FR-#, IT-#, NL-#.
  3. 3
    What have you already checked?
    Briefly describe what you already looked into — avoids back-and-forth and shows you've been proactive.
  4. 4
    What do you need — and by when?
    A status update? An action? A decision? Be direct. Include a deadline if relevant.
  5. 5
    Choose the right channel
    Operational topics → Teams channel. Formal/documentation needed → Email. Emergency → DM or phone.

Urgent or not?

The right classification helps both sides — and avoids unnecessary escalations.

Immediate / Urgent
  • Customer in showroom — delivery missing
  • Claim with escalation risk or legal threat
  • Wrong product configuration delivered
  • Technical failure in showroom
  • Safety incident or accident
  • PR-relevant situation
  • Important payment blocked
  • Order value over EUR/CHF 3,000
Normal / No rush
  • General product questions (fabric, models)
  • Orders without urgent deadline
  • Invoice clarifications (48 h)
  • Internal team organisation
  • Marketing materials request
  • HR / contract questions
  • Re-orders (covers, modules, cushions)
  • Fabric sample requests
Rule of thumb
If a customer is actively waiting and you can't give them an answer → urgent, Teams DM direct. If it can wait until tomorrow → normal Teams channel. Real emergencies: call directly or involve your showroom manager.
